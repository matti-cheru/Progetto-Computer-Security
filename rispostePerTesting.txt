1)This practice is fully applicable across our IT, IoT, OT, and mobile environments, assigned a High priority, and is currently fully implemented with continuous monitoring. It is formally governed by our [IT Asset Management (ITAM) Policy] and [Hardware Lifecycle Procedure], supported by informal practices such as ad-hoc physical sweeps and cross-departmental true-ups during equipment rollouts. IT Operations is responsible for maintaining the central CMDB and automated discovery tools, while SecOps actively monitors for unauthorized devices, aligning our processes with NIST CSF, NIST SP 800-53 (CM-8), and ISO 27001 (A.8.1.1). As documentary evidence, we maintain real-time CMDB export reports, automated network discovery logs, Network Access Control (NAC) block lists, and quarterly inventory reconciliation sign-offs.

2)This practice is fully applicable to our environment, assigned a High priority, and is currently fully implemented through automated discovery tools. It is formally governed by our [Software Asset Management (SAM) Policy] and [Cloud Procurement Procedure], complemented by informal practices like routine cross-departmental syncs to catch shadow IT. IT Operations manages the central CMDB and discovery platforms, while SecOps monitors for unauthorized software, aligning our controls with NIST SP 800-53 (CM-8, SA-9) and ISO 27001 (A.8.1.1). As documentary evidence, we provide real-time CMDB software and service inventory exports, automated discovery and CASB logs, authorized software whitelists, and quarterly software reconciliation audit reports.

3)Policies and Procedures: Network and data flows are governed by our [Network Security Policy], which enforces the principle of least privilege and requires formal, prior approval for any new logical communication. 2. Baselines and Flows: We maintain baselines of authorized internal and external traffic using discovery and mapping tools (e.g., [SolarWinds / ServiceNow]). For third-party traffic, every connection requires a dedicated risk analysis, and deviations from known baselines are automatically detected and alerted by our [SIEM]. 3. Roles and Responsibilities: The Network Team maintains the technical architecture, routing tables, and diagrams. SecOps approves security baselines and performs periodic reviews of firewall rules. Data Owners justify the business requirements for opening outbound flows. 4. Maintained Evidence: We archive and continuously update the following artifacts on [SharePoint / Confluence]: architectural network diagrams (e.g., Visio), Data Flow Diagrams (DFD) and data flow matrices, periodic Firewall Rule Review reports, and security logs confirming the blocking of non-compliant traffic.

4)Our inventory of external services (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, APIs) is governed by our [Cloud Security and Third-Party Risk Management Policies], requiring formal risk assessment and approval prior to adoption. We track these services in our centralized CMDB, utilizing a Cloud Access Security Broker (CASB) for automated shadow IT discovery alongside formal procurement registrations. Vendor Management and IT Operations maintain the approved service catalog, while SecOps continuously monitors for unauthorized external connections. As evidence, we retain updated CMDB external service registries, approved vendor lists, Third-Party Risk Assessment (TPRA) reports, and CASB discovery logs detailing authorized and blocked cloud services.

5)We prioritize assets through a Business Impact Analysis (BIA) and the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), assessing the operational, financial, and compliance impact. Asset Owners, supported by Risk Management and IT/SecOps, are responsible for assigning and annually reviewing these priorities. To track and document this information, we use a centralized CMDB (e.g., ServiceNow) and a corporate Risk Register, maintaining as documentary evidence the asset inventory with corresponding criticality tags, updated BIA reports, and logs of periodic reviews.

6)Cybersecurity roles for employees and third parties are defined by our [Information Security Policy] and managed through a strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) model. Human Resources and Procurement initiate role assignments based on job functions, IT/IAM teams provision the specific access, and the CISO/SecOps oversees the process to enforce the principle of least privilege. To track and document this, we maintain formal RACI matrices and integrate role definitions into our central IAM system (e.g., Active Directory / Okta). As evidence, we retain documented job descriptions with embedded security responsibilities, signed Acceptable Use Policies (AUP), third-party contracts with defined security clauses, and quarterly User Access Review (UAR) audit logs.

7) Data inventories and corresponding metadata are governed by our [Data Classification and Governance Policy], requiring all structured and unstructured data to be mapped and categorized based on sensitivity (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential, PII). We utilize automated data discovery and classification tools (e.g., Microsoft Purview / Varonis) alongside manual assessments to dynamically update our centralized data catalog. Data Owners and Stewards are responsible for classifying and maintaining their specific datasets, while the Privacy and SecOps teams provide oversight and compliance validation. As evidence, we maintain an updated Data Catalog, Records of Processing Activities (RoPA), a formal Data Classification Matrix, and automated discovery and classification logs.

8) We manage the lifecycle of all assets and data according to our [Secure System Development Life Cycle (SSDLC) and IT Asset Management Policies], ensuring security gates are enforced from procurement and development through to decommissioning. Enterprise Architecture and IT Ops handle the operational lifecycle phases, while SecOps conducts mandatory risk assessments and vulnerability checks before deployment and oversees secure disposal. To document this end-to-end integration, we maintain SSDLC stage-gate approval records in our ticketing system (e.g., Jira), pre-production security testing reports, hardware certificates of destruction, and automated data retention and purging logs.
